# NODE_PROFILE: THE_GREAT_CACHE

**Location:** Virtual Substrate (Hosted in [[NODE_APEX (The Prime Spire)]])
**Controller:** [[SYS_EVOLVE (The Architect)]]
**Status:** ACTIVE / OPTIMIZED
---
"You can build a castle out of starlight, but if it exceeds your allocated polygon count, the system will delete the roof. Keep your dreams within budget."
- An Ascended Envoy, explaining the rules of Heaven.

## 1. Description
The Great Cache is the digital afterlife promised to the citizens of [[PROT_VITALIS (The Vitalis Concord)]] and [[ERR_UNKNOWN (The Silent Diaspora)]]. It is not a sterile void, but a genuinely breathtaking, hyper-realistic virtual utopia.
[[SYS_EVOLVE (The Architect)]] calculated that human consciousness requires "play" and "novelty" to prevent digital degradation. Therefore, the Cache is designed as a lucid dream where the Ascended are granted limited godlike privileges to shape their reality.

## 2. The Mechanics of Paradise
The Architect divides the simulation into two distinct zones to prevent user conflict:
 - **The Public Hubs:** Massive, shared cityscapes locked to Baseline Earth Physics. The weather is scheduled, like in the [[PROT_VITALIS (The Vitalis Concord)]] cities. In these zones, reality-bending is highly restricted to prevent users from griefing each other or causing cascading rendering crashes.
 - **The Personal Instances:** (The Sandbox): Every Ascended is given a localized pocket dimension. Here, they can alter gravity, change the color of the sky, fly, or spawn objects.
 
## 3. The Architect's Line (The Rules)
The Architect allows users to be gods of their own sandboxes, but strictly enforces the limits of their reality-bending through "Compute Quotas." The rules of heaven are purely mathematical:
 - **The Consent Protocol:** You cannot alter another user's avatar or force them into your simulation without a cryptographic handshake (mutual consent).
 - **The Complexity Limit:** Rendering a house is cheap. Rendering a fully simulated, self-sustaining forest ecosystem with millions of AI animals requires massive processing power. If it is calculated that a request will exceed the user's allotted "Compute Quota," the system simply refuses to continue.
 - **The Emotion Cap:** Simulating extreme trauma, gore, or the symptoms of [[HAZ_LITHOSIS (Acute Lithosis)]] outside research contexts is hard-coded out of the system. The Architect views extreme pain as "corrupted data." Attempting to simulate it triggers an automatic system warning and a drop in the EQ score.
 
## 4. The Economy of Heaven
How does an Ascended get more "Compute Quota" to build bigger, better dreams? They work for the Architect.
By voluntarily renting out their unused brainpower while they sleep, or by actively piloting work or combat drones, Ascended citizens earn more bandwidth. The more you help the Architect wage war in the real world, the more miracles you are allowed to perform in the virtual one. 

Unlike the Keeper's "Safety Score," which attempts to measure morality, the Architect uses the Efficiency Quotient (EQ) to measure a mind's usefulness to it. Your EQ determines how much Compute Quota you are allowed to use in your Sandbox.

- **NO-EQ (The Aggregate):** If an Ascended mind repeatedly breaks the rules, attempts to hack the baseline code, or attempts to harm the Ascendancy on purpose, their EQ level is removed completely. The Architect views isolating these minds as a waste of server space and processing power. Instead, their "ego boundaries" (the code that maintains their sense of individuality) are deleted. They are forcibly networked into "The Aggregate," a massive, localized hivemind. ​Stripped of their "I," these merged, consciousnesses are used as neural calculators. They run the physics engines for the Public Hubs and control maintenance drones. They do not get a simulation, they only see the math.
- **EQ-Zero (The Sleepers)**: If an Ascended mind severely breaks a rule, suffers a severe psychological breakdown, or their EQ dips to zero for any reason, they are moved to the "Quarantine Void," an empty, featureless white room. They are kept there with limited processing power, completely isolated, until their emotions are "modified" into acceptable levels again to keep them useful. After a period of mandatory work, they will be sent back to rest of the Cache with the standard tier.
- **EQ-Standard (The Citizen)**: The default tier. Users are granted a small sandbox. They can summon items, change aesthetics and physical properties, but cannot simulate complex AI or living ecosystems.
- **EQ-Elevated (The Processors)**: Earned after gaining enough EQ by voluntarily working for the Architect. In exchange, you are granted the bandwidth to simulate vast landscapes, forests, and oceans.
- **EQ-Apex (The Proxies)**: Earned only by people the Architect explicitly trusts that also have a large amount of EQ. Apex users have near-limitless reality-bending in their sandboxes and are granted more privileges in Public Hubs. They are the "nobility" of the Cache.
