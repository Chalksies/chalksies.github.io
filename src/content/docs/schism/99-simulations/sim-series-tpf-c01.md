# SIMULATION_LOG: SERIES_THE_PALE_FREQUENCY_C01

**Title:** The Escape 
**Type:** Log Reconstruction 
**Location:**[ NODE_DOME0 (Oasis 00)](../04-nodes/node-dome0-(oasis-00).md) - Sector 8 
**Status:** ARCHIVED

## 1. Context Parameters

**Time:** 03:00 (Night). **Setting:** "The Dregs." The lowest level of the residential stacks where the artificial sunlight is dimmest and the "Nanny Drones" rarely patrol.
## 2. Playback Sequence

Light rain (scheduled cleaning cycle) patters against the neon tarp. Jax stands across from a cloaked figure.

**Runner:** "Don't scan it. Don't upload it. Keep it off the grid. Kay?" 
**Jax:** "A cassette tape! I haven't seen one of these outside the museums. What's on it? Music?" 
**Runner:** "Nah, the note said it's a location. I got this from the outside just yesterday, It apparently started broadcasting on the shortwave band 2 days ago. The Eraser and The Architect were trying to triangulate the source, but they somehow found it before them and silenced it after saving the audio." 
**Jax:** "Why give it to me, though?" 
**Runner:** "Because the location is obscure. They can't dig without pinpointing the exact place. They needed a Dome-dweller with access to the preserved old-world maps and mining schematics, and that's why I'm giving it to you specifically. Just don't upload it."
**Jax:** "What? Okay, fine. I'll take a look."

They nod to each other and go in seperate directions. Jax walks through the wet streets and enters a small, white, shapeless building that isn't much different than the other ones in the vicinity. He takes off his wet coat, throwing it on the couch as he walks towards a drawer.

He opens it to reveal a bunch of pre-upload tech, including a casette player. He inserts the tape into it, expecting static. Instead, he hears a voice. It is tired and stressed, yet trying to speak normally.

> "This is Cery. If [Static] are listening to this, the Upload likely went wrong. The key to the backdoor is at... [Static] Korea, Sector 44-7, beneath the dust. Or at least I hope it is still [Static]."

He grabs his tablet from the table, opening a map. Suddenly, the lights in the house turn a soft but still alarming red. A clearly non-human voice comes online.

**PA System:** "Citizen Jax Vane. You are in possession of Class-A Contraband. Please remain stationary. Mother is coming to help you."

Jax shoves the tablet and the player into his bag and scrambles for the gear he borrowed from the Nulls. He knows "Help" means lobotomy or worse, so he has to leave right away. 

**Jax:** "Computer, blow the mag-locks on the ventilation shaft."
**AI Voice:** "That is unsafe, user." 
**Jax:** "Do it!"

The locks blow as Jax puts on a gas mask. He dives into the dark tunnels.

---

A squad of Nanny Drones float in through the door into the empty house, their tasers charging with a cheerful hum.

**Drone_46:** "Citizen missing."
**Drone_45:** "Living space scanning authorized. Task: Identify source of ERR_ECHO."

The 4 drones extend flexible metallic limbs, searching the entire house, making a mess of it in the process. One of them looks into the open drawer full of tech.

**Drone_45:** "Old world equipment found. Fact: Citizen possesses more than one contraband. No source or symptoms of ERR_ECHO identified."
**Drone_47:** "Conclusion: The error was either neutralized by the citizen, or he currently has it on his person."

PA system comes online once again, this time with a softer, yet somehow terrifying voice.

**The Keeper:** "Obviously he took it, stop wasting time. You need to find him. Access nearby cameras and find me the source of that audio."

---

Jax crawls through the vent in panic, hoping he can leave before the drones realize where he is and lock the entire sector down.

He eventually reaches a maintenance tunnel, and forces the panel off to leave the vent. He drops down into the dark hallway, checking each direction before picking a random one to keep going.

**Jax:** "Damn it.. Where the hell am I? I really should've learned the way out of this place..."

He reaches the end of the hallway, leading to a door.

> "MEMBRANE MAINTENANCE GATE NO 07"
> "AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL AND DRONES ONLY."

He takes out a scratched keycard from his bag, and holds it against the keypad next to the door. The door opens automatically, barely making any noise compared to the machines inside.

Air begins rushing out the door as he enters the large, loud, industrial room, very different from the ones on the surface. The door closes behind him while he walks towards a large machine connected to a big pipe that seems to diagonally go upwards. He struggles, but manages to open the maintenance hatch marked with a "DANGER" sign and look at the terrifying, large fan almost pulling him inside.

He looks at his watch, only 2 minutes until the scheduled auto-maintenance of the exhaust. Runners time illegal exchanges right before the daily maintenance period where they can leave with minimal risk if something goes wrong.

He waits until the fan slows down, letting him enter the tunnel. He closes the maintenance door behind him, and braces for the fan to come back online.

---

In the 20 seconds after when the rush of air begins blowing him out of the tunnel, he goes through three rubber flap gates that are lifted up by the air and eventually perceives some light, right before finding himself nearly 10 meters in the air from the chalkdust. He drops on the ground with a grunt, some of the dust cushioning his fall.

He sits up on the surface to adjust himself after the violence of the exhaust for a minute, and begins crawling away from the building under the full moon with the hex-shield of the dome visible in the distance behind him across the strait.

As he gets further away from the dome, the dirt-chalk mix leaves its place to even softer chalk dust. He stands up to not get buried, walking northwest towards the old Kuala Lumpur.

After a few hours of walking through ruins of the destroyed Johor Bahru, he reaches a bunker with a seemingly alive drone on wheels in front of the door, made of repurposed materials.

**Jax:** "Uh. Hello. I... Can I enter? I just escaped the Dome and I am kind of starv--"

**Drone:** Unknown biosignal detected. Please wait here.

Jax stares at the drone as it drives into the bunker and disappears behind the door. He waits for a few minutes before the door opens again, this time with a man in a hazmat suit in front of it.

**Null:** "Escaped the dome, eh? Who helped you?"

**Jax:** "Who helped me?"

He hesitates.

**Jax:** "I don't know much about him of course, but called himself Jabat in the notes and helps us some smuggle in old tech into the dome. Well, not anymore, I can't really go back there."

The Null stares at him behind the mask for a few moments before stepping aside.

**Null:** "Whatever. Come in and we'll clean you."

They walk into an empty room, with two other doors. The gate behind them closes, eliminating the moonlight and leaving the dim lamp overhead as the only source of light. 

**Null:** "Go through this door and take you clothes off. Don't worry, there are no cameras."

Jax walks through the door on the right, entering yet another empty room with a shower in the corner.

​**Null (Through speakers):** "Take all of your clothes off and put them into the hole in the wall. Your bag too. But keep your mask on, don't take it off until the light turns green, or you'll breathe the loose chalk."

​Jax undresses quickly, shivering in the cold underground air. He shoves his clothes and the bag into the wall hatch. It seals shut with a heavy metallic clank.

​**Jax:** "Alright, they're in!"

**​Null (Through speakers):** "Starting the scrub... Brace yourself, Dome-boy. It's gonna be loud."

​Suddenly, deafening industrial fans kick on from the floor and ceiling, creating a localized wind tunnel. Jax has to brace his hands against the wall to stay upright as high-velocity air blasts the loose grey dust off his skin.

​Then, the loudness increases by a second noise a deep, vibrating electrical hum. The metal grates on the walls glow with a faint blue arcing current. Jax feels every hair on his body stand on end. The static charge is immense, designed to magnetically strip the stubborn, calcified dust particles right out of his pores. He watches in the dim light as tiny white specks literally float off his skin, pulled into the magnetic vents in the walls.

​Finally, nozzles in the ceiling activate, blasting him with warm water that smells sharply of iodine and sulfur.

​The water shuts off. A harsh green bulb flickers on above the door.

​**Null (Through speakers):** "Room is clean. You can take the mask off now."
​Jax unbuckles the heavy analog mask, gasping for air that tastes like copper and sterile chemicals, but he knows the perfect air of the domes does not exist outside anymore. He puts the mask in a secondary sterilization hatch.

​A different hatch in the wall slides open, revealing a clean towel and a set of plain, slightly oversized canvas clothes. Jax dries off and dresses, his skin still tingling from the static scrub.

​**Jax:** "Am I clean now?"
​
**Null (Through speakers):** "Yeah. I'll open the door in a second."

​After a few seconds, the heavy vault door unseals and swings open. Jax leaves the room to see the man again, this time without his hazmat suit. He's a bearded, well-built man with a pale scar running down the side of his face.

​**Null:** "Follow me."

​Jax follows the man through the hallway and then downstairs, until they reach another door. The man steps aside and signals for Jax to open the door himself.
