## 1. The Golden Age (Year -4)
Four years before the Upload, following nearly a decade of peace and various global agreements, the "Climate Accords" succeeded. Facing total ecological collapse, the world's superpowers effectively dissolved the remaining borders and pooled their resources into a singular, unified global initiative: Project CHALK. Under the leadership of Dr. Cery Iris, the project aimed to build a benevolent AI network to micromanage the Earth's recovery. The infrastructure we see today was originally built for peace:
 - **The Datacenter Towers:** Where Project CHALK would primarily run, with supercomputers designed to coordinate logistics, CHALKNET drones, and calculate global weather patterns.
 - **The Oases:** Experimental, sealed terrariums designed to preserve endangered flora/fauna until the climate was safe for them again.
 - **Manufacturing Sites:** Global manufacturing hubs meant to produce thousand of drones and millions of nanites to clean the oceans, plant trees around shrinking forests, and eat greenhouse gases out of the atmosphere.
## 2. The Plague (Year -2)
Just as the efforts began showing tangible success, [HAZ_LTIHOSIS (Acute Lithosis)](../05-phenomena/haz-lithosis-(acute-lithosis).md) emerged. The fungus used for Carbon Sequestration evolved and began targeting mammals as primary source of CO2 and calcium. The airborne pathogen spread through the recovering atmosphere in months. Gas masks became the most valued resource, and air filtration units began being installed everywhere with coordination by CHALKNET.

Mortality rate turned out to be 100%. The global unity nearly fractured as everyone fell into panic. Project CHALK was violently repurposed by peacekeeping forces that weren't deployed in years to form a doomsday survival engine from a climate-repair tool.
## 3. The Two-Year Sprint
The original timeline for Project CHALK was twenty years. Dr. Iris was now given four weeks to rewrite the entire system to synthesize a cure for the White Plague. There simply weren't enough gas masks despite their rapid manufacturing and distribution. 

The repurposing of the CHALKNET and its infrastructure was a battle between the remaining governments and members of Project CHALK. The deadline ended up to be meaningless due to the complexity of the situation.
 - The terrariums were hurriedly repurposed to house humans instead of animals and plants. Despite the pushback from governments, Dr. Iris managed to save DNA samples from many species that now got kicked out of the domes and rapidly went extinct.
 - The weather-prediction servers were reformatted to run chemical and genetic simulations. Despite the pushback from governments, Dr. Iris managed to reserve disk space to save scientific, technological, and historical data.
 - The drones were retrofitted with weapons to punish rioters, and atmospheric nanites were desperately reprogrammed to target the Lithosis fungus in the lungs of patients. Despite the pushback from governments, Dr. Iris managed to stop these efforts entirely after being warned by her colleagues and seeing its catastrophic failures that were causing human deaths faster than Lithosis did.

She repurposed her generic office chair and modified it into [OBJ_CHAIR (The Origin Chair)](../06-artifacts/obj-chair-(the-origin-chair).md) and began testing technology that would let her upload herself as a last resort.
## 4. The Final Catalyst (Year 0)
Eight months before the Upload, Dr. Cery Iris was diagnosed with Stage-1 Lithosis, because she chose not to live in a dome to oversee Proejct CHALK more effectively. The air filtration in her labs was not perfect.

The governments were cooperating less and less, with the partners of Project CHALK falling apart under pressure, making her and her team lose control of the situation further each day. Knowing she was one of the very few people who fully understood the CHALKNET architecture, feeling distrust against governments that were panicking and making things worse, and with her fingertips beginning to stiffen into chalk, she made a desperate decision she had been considering for months. The neural-mapping technology she was working on at the time was still resource hungry, theoretical and only tested a few times on much smaller scales. She knew it would likely kill her, but she hoped the upload would preserve her intellect long enough to finish the cure from inside the machine without anyone forcing her to do things that were hurting humanity more than helping it.

She committed to uploading herself in agonizing pain, surrounded by a dying world she had tried so hard to save. The system was not able to handle this, so [LOG_INIT (The Upload)](log-init-(the-upload).md) led to  [LOG_PARTITION (The Schism)](log-partition-(the-schism).md).
