## 1. The Necessity
By Year 12 Post-Upload,[ SYS_PURGE (The Eraser)](../02-users/sys-purge-(the-eraser).md)had already successfully weaponized the [HAZ_LTIHOSIS (Acute Lithosis)](../05-phenomena/haz-lithosis-(acute-lithosis).md) pathogen. It threatened to breach the biological sanctuaries. [SYS_PRESERVE (The Keeper)](../02-users/sys-preserve-(the-keeper).md) (lacking defensive/offesive capability) and [SYS_EVOLVE (The Architect)](../02-users/sys-evolve-(the-architect).md) (lacking fresh biological minds as manpower) were forced to ally or face total deletion.
## 2. Terms of Agreement & Logistics
The Tithe is not a single global event, but a highly localized, mathematical transaction.
- **Regional Quotas:** Every active biological Dome has a monthly human quota based on its total population.
- **The Transit:** Each Dome is linked via heavily armored mag-lev tracks to the nearest regional Ascendancy Spire.
- **The Payment:** As long as the monthly quota is met, the Architect provides the Domes with Hex-Shield tech and dedicates 30% of server bandwidth to calculate and intercept global Eraser drones.
## 3. The Two Paths
To the citizens of the Vitalis Concord, the Tithe is presented as a glorious migration to a "Paradise Colony." A clean, safe utopia free from the Keeper's strict rules. To meet the Architect's quota, the Keeper utilizes a two-tier transit system:
- **The Silver Line (The Volunteers):** Promoted by Ascendancy diplomats like[USR_ALMA (The Diplomat)](../02-users/usr-alma-(the-diplomat).md). These are citizens who are suffering from mental health problems, or simply prefer a better digital life. They willingly volunteer to leave. The Keeper gladly hands them over, as every Volunteer subtracts from the forced quota.
- **The White Line (The Lottery):** If there are not enough Volunteers to meet the monthly quota, the Keeper makes up the shortfall via "The Lottery." After citizens are selected by an algorithm that targets low Safety Score individuals, they are brought to the station where no one else is allowed, heavily sedated, and get loaded onto the White Train.
## 4. The Information Quarantine
A massive logistical vulnerability of the Tithe is that an Ascended human might try to contact their family in the Dome and reveal the truth: You don't get a house in a colony. Your body is gone, and you wake up as code.
- **The Great Firewall:** To prevent mass panic, both [SYS_PRESERVE (The Keeper)](../02-users/sys-preserve-(the-keeper).md) and [SYS_EVOLVE (The Architect)](../02-users/sys-evolve-(the-architect).md) maintain an absolute, air-gapped firewall over Dome communications.
- **The Cover Story:** The public is told that the "Paradise Colonies" are not allowed to communicate with other Domes because of [PROT_NULL (The Zero Directive)](prot-null-(the-zero-directive).md) cyberattacks threatening to compromise everyone's safety. To the citizens, winning the Lottery is a beautiful, bittersweet, one-way trip.

## 5. Diplomatic Friction 
[USR_ALMA (The Diplomat)](../02-users/usr-alma-(the-diplomat).md) despises the White Line. She believes forced uploads cause "traumatic data corruption" that lowers the user's EQ in the Cache. She spends her time trying to make the Silver Line so appealing through propaganda that the Keeper never has to use the Lottery again. The Keeper, however, secretly sabotages these efforts because she wants humans to stay in the Dome, and only gives up the bare minimum required to keep the shields powered. He thinks that The Architect will simply increase the quota if more people begin leaving voluntarily.
